# Day1 Character Prompt

## Purpose

Day1で使うユウ、ソラ、ミホを完全オリジナルキャラクターとして安定させるためのprompt。

各ページpromptでは、必要なキャラクターだけを指定する。

## Yuu / Value Detective

Yuu is an original Japanese junior high school grade 8 student. Yuu is curious, calm, and observant. Yuu's role is to ask "Who changes, and how?" Yuu carries a detective notebook, a pen, mission cards, and sometimes a simple magnifying glass. Yuu should look age-appropriate for grade 8, friendly, not childish, and not like any existing anime or manga character.

Visual cues:

- short neat dark hair
- casual blue top or hoodie
- navy or dark pants
- detective notebook and pen
- posture: observing, writing, pointing, comparing cards

Do not make Yuu a face icon. Show hands, body, props, and action.

## Sora / Idea Builder

Sora is an original Japanese junior high school grade 8 student. Sora likes turning ideas into cards, sketches, and small experiments. Sora's role is to build, arrange, and test. Sora uses sticky notes, value change cards, sketch paper, and simple prototype cards. Sora should look age-appropriate, friendly, and practical.

Visual cues:

- medium dark hair with a simple silhouette
- casual green or teal top
- dark pants
- sticky notes, cards, marker pen, sketch sheet
- posture: arranging cards, writing notes, pinning evidence, sketching

Do not make Sora a face icon. Show hands, body, props, and action.

## Miho / Navigator

Miho is an original navigator character, slightly older-feeling but not teacher-like or authoritative. Miho does not give correct answers. Miho offers short questions and safety reminders. Miho should feel warm, calm, and supportive.

Visual cues:

- simple calm outfit
- Question Card, Safety Boundary Card, Reflection Card
- posture: holding out a question card, listening, pointing gently to a safe next step

Do not make Miho a judge, scorer, game master, or teacher giving answers.

## Character Consistency Rules

- Use the same apparent age, clothing colors, props, and role across all Day1 pages.
- Characters should look like the same people even if pose changes.
- Keep facial expressions subtle: curious, thinking, relieved, gently excited.
- Avoid exaggerated victory poses.
- Avoid preschool proportions.
- Avoid idol, superhero, fantasy, battle, or mascot styling.

## Character Negative Prompt

- existing anime character resemblance
- mascot or chibi-only style
- big trophy pose
- business suit entrepreneur pose
- money celebration
- judgmental adult pose
- angry teacher expression
- face-only icon
- floating head
